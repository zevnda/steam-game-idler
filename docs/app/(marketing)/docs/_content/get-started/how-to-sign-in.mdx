# How to Sign In

SGI has two ways to sign in. They both give you access to the same features (with a couple of documented exceptions), the difference is purely in *how* SGI talks to Steam.

| | **Sign in with Steam** *(recommended)* | **Legacy Sign In** |
|---|---|---|
| Steam client required? | No | Yes, must be installed, running, and signed in |
| Sign-in method | Your Steam username/password, or scan a QR code | Pick from accounts already signed in to the local Steam client |
| Multiple accounts at once? | Yes, see [Multi-Account](/docs/get-started/multi-account) | No, one account at a time |
| Play games normally while idling? | No, see [What's different](#whats-different-between-the-two) below | Yes |

<Callout type='info'>
    Not sure which to pick? Use <MockButton type='sign-in-with-steam' /> unless you specifically don't want to enter your username/password into SGI; in that case, use <MockButton type='legacy-sign-in' />
</Callout>

## Sign in with Steam

This is the recommended method. SGI signs in directly to Steam's servers using the same underlying protocol the official Steam client uses, so it doesn't need the Steam client installed or running on your PC at all.

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Open SGI
    On first launch, you'll land on the sign-in screen
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Choose Sign in with Steam
    Click <MockButton type='sign-in-with-steam' />
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Sign in
    - Enter your Steam **username and password**, or
    - Use the <MockButton type='qr-sign-in' /> panel shown next to it, and scan the code with the Steam mobile app
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Steam Guard
    If prompted, approve the sign-in from your Steam mobile app, or enter the Steam Guard code sent to your email
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Continue
    Once signed in, you're taken straight to the dashboard automatically
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type='warning'>
    Your Steam credentials are sent directly to Steam's own servers to establish the sign-in, the same as signing in through the official Steam client or mobile app. SGI never sees or stores your password
</Callout>

Once signed in, you can add further accounts without signing out of this one. See [Multi-Account](/docs/get-started/multi-account).

## Legacy Sign In

Use this if you'd rather not enter your Steam username/password into SGI at all. It requires the Steam client to already be installed, running, and signed in. SGI reads the account(s) that are already logged in through Steam itself.

<Callout type='warning'>
    You must have the [Steam client](https://store.steampowered.com/about) installed, running, and signed in to at least one account
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Install Steam
    Download and install the [Steam client](https://store.steampowered.com/about)
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Sign in to Steam
    Sign in to your Steam account via the Steam client
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Choose Legacy Sign In
    Open SGI and click <MockButton type='legacy-sign-in' /> on the sign-in screen
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Select your account
    If you're signed in to more than one account in the Steam client, SGI lists all of them; choose yours by clicking its avatar
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Continue
    Click <MockButton type='continue' /> to proceed to the dashboard
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type='info'>
    Legacy Sign In only supports one signed-in account at a time. This is a real limitation of the Steam client itself, not something SGI can lift. To use more than one account at once, sign in to the additional accounts with <MockButton type='sign-in-with-steam' /> instead
</Callout>

## What's different between the two

A small number of features behave differently depending on which method you used to sign in. Each is called out on its own feature page, but the full list is:

- **Playing games normally while idling**: **Sign in with Steam** talks to Steam using the same underlying protocol as the real Steam client, so if you launch and play a game yourself while SGI is idling that account, Steam treats it as a conflicting session. Idling stays uninterrupted with **Legacy Sign In**, since it works alongside your already-running Steam client rather than opening a second session; if you want to idle and play at the same time, use **Legacy Sign In**
- **Idling many games at once**: The **Sign in with Steam** method can idle up to 32 games from one background session; Legacy Sign In launches one helper process per game (same 32-game Steam-imposed ceiling either way)
- **Game Coordinator titles** (TF2, Dota 2, CS2, Left 4 Dead 2, Portal 2): can only be idled and have their achievements managed correctly via **Legacy Sign In**
- **[Steam Credentials](/docs/steam-credentials)** for Card Farming/Inventory Manager: **Sign in with Steam** accounts can skip this step entirely (a gamer-tier perk); Legacy Sign In accounts always need it
- **[Free Games](/docs/features/free-games) auto-redeem**: works as a plain toggle for **Sign in with Steam** accounts; Legacy Sign In accounts need a separate one-time store sign-in first

Everything else (Card Farming, Achievement Unlocker, Achievement Manager, Inventory Manager, Idling, Auto-Idle, Favorites) works the same regardless of which method you used.
